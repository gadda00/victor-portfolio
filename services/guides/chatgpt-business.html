How to Use ChatGPT for Your Business

ChatGPT can write your emails, draft your proposals, answer your customers, and analyze your data — but only if you know how to ask. This guide gives you 10 copy-paste prompts, a pricing breakdown, and the privacy rules every business owner needs to know.

Why ChatGPT Matters for Small Business

The average small business now runs five AI tools in its daily operations, according to the SBE Council's 2026 report. ChatGPT is the gateway — it handles writing, research, analysis, and customer communication at a fraction of the cost of hiring for each task individually. But most business owners either avoid it entirely (leaving value on the table) or use it badly (getting generic, unusable output).

This guide fixes both problems. You'll leave with prompts you can use today, a clear understanding of which plan to buy, and the confidence that you're not leaking confidential data.

⚠️ Privacy Warning

If you're handling customer data, financial records, or confidential business information, the Free and Plus plans are not safe — OpenAI may use your inputs to train its models. The moment you touch client data, upgrade to Business ($20/seat/mo). It's the cheapest plan where your data is not used for training.

10 Copy-Paste Business Prompts

Each prompt below uses the RTF framework — Role, Task, Format. This is the single most important prompt engineering pattern: tell the AI who to be, what to do, and how to format the answer. Replace the bracketed [placeholders] with your details.

1. Cold Email

Before (generic)
Write an email to a prospect.
After (RTF — copy-paste ready)
Act as a B2B sales copywriter [Role]. Write a 90-word cold email to a [job title] at a [industry] company in [city], offering [product] that solves [pain point]. Use a one-line hook, one proof point, and a soft CTA to book a 15-min call [Task]. Format: subject line + body + P.S. [Format]

2. Project Proposal

Copy-paste ready
Act as a consultant [Role]. Draft a 1-page proposal for [client name] to deliver [scope of work] in [timeline] for [budget]. Include: problem statement, approach, deliverables, timeline table, pricing breakdown, and terms [Task]. Format as markdown with H2 headings [Format].

3. Facebook / Instagram Ad Copy

Copy-paste ready
Act as a paid-social media buyer [Role]. Write 5 Facebook ad variations for [product], targeting [audience persona]. Each ≤125 characters primary text, one emoji, clear CTA [Task]. Format: a numbered list with hook | body | CTA [Format].

4. LinkedIn Post

Copy-paste ready
Act as a thought-leadership ghostwriter [Role]. Write a 150-word LinkedIn post on [topic] in the voice of a [your role]. Open with a contrarian one-liner, 3 short paragraphs, end with a question. No hashtags in the body [Task]. Format: plain text, ready to paste [Format].

5. Customer Complaint Reply

Copy-paste ready
Act as a customer-success manager [Role]. Reply to this angry customer message: [paste message here]. Acknowledge the frustration, apologize without admitting legal fault, explain the fix, offer [compensation] [Task]. Tone: calm, professional, 80 words. Format: ready-to-send email [Format].

6. Meeting Agenda

Copy-paste ready
Act as an executive assistant [Role]. Build a 45-minute agenda for a [meeting type: weekly sync / project kickoff / quarterly review] about [topic] with [attendee roles]. Include time-boxed items, desired outcome per item, and pre-reads [Task]. Format as a markdown table [Format].

7. Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Copy-paste ready
Act as an operations manager [Role]. Write an SOP for [process name]. Include: purpose, scope, roles involved, numbered step-by-step instructions, tools required, escalation path, and version history [Task]. Format as a document with H2/H3 headings [Format].

8. Job Description

Copy-paste ready
Act as an HR manager [Role]. Write a job description for [role] at [company], [location], [salary band]. Include: about us, responsibilities (8 bullets), requirements (must-have / nice-to-have), benefits, how to apply [Task]. Bias-checked, gender-neutral language. Format: ready to post on LinkedIn [Format].

9. Performance Review

Copy-paste ready
Act as a manager [Role]. Draft a performance review for [employee name] covering [Q1/Q2/etc.]. Use this data: [paste metrics, notes, feedback]. Structure: strengths, areas to grow, goals for next quarter [Task]. Tone: specific, fair, no fluff. 250 words. Format: ready to share in 1:1 [Format].

10. 30-Second Elevator Pitch

Copy-paste ready
Act as a sales coach [Role]. Write a 30-second elevator pitch for [product/service] aimed at [buyer persona]. Structure: problem → agitate → solution → proof → ask [Task]. Plain spoken, ≤80 words, no jargon [Format].

When NOT to Use ChatGPT

ChatGPT is powerful, but it's not a lawyer, doctor, or accountant. Here's where you need a human professional:

  • Legal advice — ChatGPT can hallucinate statutes and case law. For contracts, disputes, or compliance, use a qualified advocate (in Kenya: Law Society of Kenya members).
  • Medical diagnosis — It produces plausible-sounding but potentially dangerous medical advice. The KMPDC and FDA both warn against using AI for diagnosis.
  • Financial and tax advice — ChatGPT has no fiduciary duty. Numbers can be subtly wrong. Use a CPA or KRA-registered tax advisor.
  • Anything requiring a signature, license, or regulated judgment — actuarial valuations, engineering stamps, prescriptions, audit opinions.
  • Real-time pricing or breaking news — ChatGPT's training data has a cutoff. For current information, use Perplexity (which searches the live web and cites sources).

5 Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Using Free or Plus for confidential client work — your data may train the model. Upgrade to Business.
  2. Accepting numbers and statistics without verification — ChatGPT hallucinates citations. Always fact-check.
  3. No role and no format — "Write me a proposal" gives you generic junk. Always use RTF.
  4. Pasting full contracts without an NDA-safe plan — even on Business, use judgment with highly sensitive documents.
  5. Treating output as final — always edit for voice, accuracy, and context. ChatGPT is a draft machine, not a finished-product machine.
