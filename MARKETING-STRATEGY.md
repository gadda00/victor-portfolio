## 4. Email marketing (the nurture engine)

### Newsletter setup (Substack, free to start)

**Name:** "AI, Built in Nairobi" (or "The Multi-Agent Memo")
**Cadence:** bi-weekly (issues 1 and 15 of each month)
**Format (3 sections, 600 words max):**
1. **One thing I built** — a technical learning from Busara AI / KilimoPRO
2. **One thing I read** — an African AI news item with my take
3. **One thing for you** — a free resource (guide, checklist, prompt)

**Subscription CTAs (place across the site):**
- End of every blog article
- /services/ page sidebar
- Homepage About section
- Wizard result modal ("Get a free AI audit checklist when you subscribe")

**Target:** 500 subscribers in 90 days.

### Drip sequence (for wizard completers who don't book a call)

When someone completes the wizard but doesn't book a call within 48 hrs, trigger a 4-email sequence:

| Email | Day | Subject | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | +2 | "Your AI project brief — next steps" | Recap their recommendation + link to discovery call |
| 2 | +5 | "How [similar company] used the same package" | 1 short case study |
| 3 | +9 | "Free: AI audit checklist (PDF)" | Lead magnet, no pitch |
| 4 | +14 | "Should I close your brief?" | Soft close — "I'll archive your brief Friday if I don't hear back" |

**Tool:** ConvertKit (free up to 1K subscribers) or Buttondown (simpler, $9/mo).

**Note:** The current site is static (GitHub Pages). To trigger drips, you'll need either:
- **Formspree** ($10/mo) — wire the wizard's "email" field to a Formspree endpoint, which forwards to ConvertKit
- **Netlify Forms** (free) — if you migrate hosting
- **ConvertKit inline form** embedded in the wizard result modal (simplest)
